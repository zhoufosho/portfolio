SmartCart

CS247 Instructor's Choice Award

Project URL: View Website
Source Code: View on GitHub

Android Java | Winter 2013

SmartCart is a smart shopping tablet app that guides users through a grocery store. The app helps users to locate items and allows them to purchase items at the cart.

Needfinding in this project began with talking with shoppers about supermarket frustrations, following their shopping habits, and talking with employees about self-checkout hassles and inefficiencies. SmartCart then set out to tackle two main problems.
1. Does SmartCart help users find what they need more efficiently?
2. Does SmartCart make shopping less frustrating?

Software prototypes were tested with shoppers in Safeway store. Users highlighted vague interface problems related to checkout.

Many usability issues prompted the need for a hardware encasing. Laser-cut acrylic sheets were used to make an encasing fit just right for the shopping cart.

discoveroute

CS147 Best Interaction Design CS147 Best Poster Design

Project URL: discoveroute.com
Source Code: View on GitHub

Node.js, Javascript, MongoDB, Bootstrap | Winter 2014

discoveroute is a place-discovery mobile web application to help the curious traveler explore places along routes by offering path-based destination searches. Find new places by old spaces.

Needfinding in this project began with talking with people planning their outings. Typically, people research an areas close to their starting point or end destination on Google Maps to make the most of travel time. Those who try to stop at places enroute expressed frustration due to lack of tools searching on a path between start and end.

Thus, discoveroute's aim is to help people maximize their journey exploration and optimize travel time.

Final Product


User Testing revealed a few usability issues that were fixed for the final version.

One problem was confusion in selecting categories. The original version was a chromatic display that showed a thin white border around a selected category. This was too subtle a response.

After running an A/B test through Google Analytics, number of clicks on a monochromatic redesign versus the original were measured. The monochrome version had clearer actions and resulted in less button mashing.

Brainstorm, Define, Prototype

2013 ACM UIST Poster Presenter

Paper URL: View Paper
Research Project | Spring 2013

In a group of two, I studied the role of delaying constraints in effective design methodologies. Groups presented to narrowed constraints at various stages in the design process yielded results of varying novelty and effectiveness. Results indicate that there is the possibility of 'latent design' at play, and those who brainstormed and prototyped before receiving the narrow constraints yielded results that were both more novel and more appropriate than other groups.

I presented this research project at the 2013 ACM Symposium on User Interface Software Technology in St. Andrews, Scotland.

Nautilus

Design Brief: Create a perfectly-fitting container for a given rock, and nothing else.
Product Design | Winter 2013

Nautilus is composed entirely of laser-cut 1/16" balsa wood.

I received a rock of a muted sea-green, remniscent of salty sea waves at their peak, about to crash. I wanted an open, monochrome container to highlight the color and geometric consistency of the rock.
